# Appendix E — The Lover: Aliveness, Eros, and Its Shadows

## Theoretical Background

In Moore and Gillette's system, the **Lover** is the archetype of aliveness, relatedness, and the experience of life as inherently meaningful and beautiful. He is not merely the erotic — though eros is his most visible expression. The Lover is the one who is *connected*: to other people, to the body, to the senses, to the aesthetic dimension of existence, to the feeling-tone that makes experience more than mere event. He is the archetype of empathy, passion, play, and the capacity to be moved.

Where the Warrior achieves through detachment, the Lover achieves through *attachment* — he is fully present to what is happening, susceptible to beauty and suffering alike. He makes the other three archetypes human: the King without the Lover is cold; the Warrior without the Lover is cruel; the Magician without the Lover is manipulative.

His two shadow poles are:
- **The Addicted Lover** (active shadow): the Lover without boundaries — consumed by passion, unable to contain, moving compulsively from experience to experience, never satisfied, confusing intensity for depth
- **The Impotent Lover** (passive shadow): the Lover cut off from feeling — numb, depressed, detached from the body, unable to connect, experiencing the world as flat and meaningless

---

## The Lover in Full Expression

The Lover is present throughout the corpus in two distinct but related registers: sensory-erotic and aesthetic-spiritual. The two are not separated in this dreamscape — the body's aliveness and the soul's aliveness are consistently treated as continuous rather than opposed.

### The Sensory Register

**Morning Dew** is the Lover archetype's most concentrated expression in the diary. The dream is immersive, multi-sensory, richly erotic:

> *"In a beautiful glade with morning dew... licks dew off plants... a voluptuous red-haired woman pleasures the dreamer, then they make love."*

This is the Lover at his healthiest in the purely sensory mode: fully present, delighting in every texture and taste (the dew on the plants, the woman's body, the quality of the morning light). The post-dream note acknowledges the power of this: the dreamer is "curious and craves seeing her again." This is not addiction — it is the healthy Lover's response to genuine erotic encounter, the desire for continuation rather than escalation.

The morning dew itself is the Lover's characteristic image: the threshold moment (dawn, the liminal hour), the smallest-scale beauty (dew is water in its most intimate form, at the boundary between leaf and air), the transient. The Lover attends to what is fleeting precisely because he is fully present to the moment rather than managing it from a distance.

### The Aesthetic-Spiritual Register

The lucid dream sequences across the corpus show the Lover in his aesthetic-spiritual mode — the capacity to be fully alive to the texture of the dreamscape:

**Heavenly Ascension**: "a golden circle of symbols and faces, beautiful song... tingling and feeling of living water coursing through body." The Lover's response to the sacred is physical — tingling, coursing water, the body registering the divine through sensation.

**Confession Of Calling To Dad**: during the lucid Edinburgh-like cityscape, "the dreamer delights in touching everything while singing and moving." Touching everything — the Lover's characteristic gesture, wanting direct physical contact with the world rather than visual survey from a safe distance.

**The Restaurant**: "reading advertising, touching walls" in a beautiful procedural city. The rubbing of surfaces that anchors the dreamer in lucid dreams is a Lover practice — staying present through touch, refusing the dissociation that would reduce the dreamscape to spectacle.

**Prayers Of The Saints**: the "breathtaking city with gardens, trees, streams, waterfalls, beautiful buildings" is the Lover's ideal environment — the City of God as an aesthetic totality, beautiful in every direction simultaneously.

## The Lover and the Body as Spiritual Territory

One of the most important Lover themes in the corpus is the treatment of the body as a legitimate spiritual territory rather than an obstacle to transcendence.

**Heavenly Ascension**'s underwater prayer — breathing beneath the surface while saying the Jesus Prayer — is a Lover act: the body fully immersed, not transcended. The living water coursing through the body during the heavenly ascent is not metaphor but sensation. The divine is encountered bodily, not despite the body.

**Morning Dew**'s interpretation acknowledges the ambiguity: the dreamer wonders whether the encounter embodies "yearning for the feminine as guardian of sweet ambrosia and the role of sex/lust in spiritual slumber." The Lover's awareness is present: sensory delight and spiritual sleepiness are related, and the diary does not pretend otherwise. The Lover who is only erotic, who never moves beyond sensory satisfaction to the relational depth it requires, is at risk of the Addicted Lover's trajectory.

The dreamer's comment that he was "tempted to pornography afterward" is the Addicted Lover's shadow appearing at the edge of the healthy encounter: the impulse to repeat the intensity through a substitute when the real encounter is over.

## The Addicted Lover Shadow

The Addicted Lover — boundaryless, compulsive, intensity-seeking — appears in its most contained form in the post-*Morning Dew* pornography temptation. The structure is classic: a genuine Lover-encounter (the red-haired woman in the glade) generates desire; the desire, when the encounter ends, seeks satisfaction through a substitute that provides the sensation without the relationship. This is the Addicted Lover's logic: detach the pleasure from the person and multiply the input.

**Breaking Out**'s barbed-penis imagery and its boar-person transformation suggest the Addicted Lover's shadow breaking through the body when the ego cannot contain the erotic charge through conscious engagement. The boar is the animal Lover — sexually charged, physically transformed, ungoverned by relational context or consent. The barbed quality specifically indicates the way uncontained erotic energy becomes harmful: not the clean desire of the healthy Lover but something that injures in the very act of contact.

The garden succubi of **Temptation In The Garden** can be read through the Addicted Lover frame: they are pure sensory-erotic without relational substance, their demand ("let our men in for the seed of Adam") transactional rather than connective. The Addicted Lover would yield — the intensity is real and the offer is pleasurable. The dreamer's refusal is the Warrior's discipline operating within the Lover's territory: maintaining the boundary without denying the desire.

## The Impotent Lover Shadow

The Impotent Lover — numb, cut off, unable to connect — is present in the corpus primarily as a threat rather than as an achieved state. Several dreams suggest the dreamer is aware of the risk of spiritual experience becoming dissociated from embodied feeling.

**Future Bugs** contains the most direct reference: filled with dread at his task (bringing revelations to a parallel world without ruining the existing balance), the dreamer closes the hatch on a beautiful humanoid male and "decides to step into the filth of the world." The post-hospitalization note adds: "machine life and insects were preoccupations." The Impotent Lover's characteristic failure is the refusal of embodied, messy, relational engagement — the retreat into the clean sphere of the ideal. Choosing to step into the filth is the choice *against* Impotent Lover dissociation.

**Depression Injection** in **Jesus Is Lord** — the menacing figures offering an injection that would produce depression — is the Impotent Lover's shadow as external threat: something that would sever the dreamer from feeling, cut the Lover connection deliberately. The dreamer runs from it. This is significant: the injection is not merely harmful, it is specifically depressive — it targets aliveness, the Lover's domain.

The **Supermarket of Sin**'s atmosphere — "frantic, depressing... desperate frenzy" — describes a world in which the Lover has been colonised by the Addicted Lover (frantic consumption) while the authentic Lover-connection (nourishment, pleasure, community) is completely absent. The staff hate the customers; the customers cannot stop consuming the rubbish that makes them hungrier. This is both Addicted Lover and Impotent Lover operating simultaneously: the drive to consume without satisfaction (Addicted) producing the numbing dissatisfaction of the Impotent register.

---

## The Lover and Contemplative Practice

The Jesus Prayer practice during lucid dreams is, among its other functions, a Lover practice: it maintains full sensory and emotional presence within the spiritual experience rather than allowing it to become abstract. The prayer is repeated bodily — it has rhythm, breath, the sound of a name — and the dreamer uses it to stay anchored in the quality of the experience rather than floating off into pure observation.

The Lover is the archetype most endangered by spiritual development conducted entirely in the head. The corpus's insistence on the body — touching surfaces, breathing water, licking dew, feeling living water course through the body — is the Lover's resistance to a spirituality that would leave him behind.

## Summary

| Lover Mode | Expression | Dream |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Positive: Sensory fullness | Licking dew, erotic morning encounter | *Morning Dew* |
| Positive: Aesthetic presence | Touching everything while singing | *Confession Of Calling To Dad* |
| Positive: Embodied sacred | Living water coursing through body | *Heavenly Ascension* |
| Positive: Stepping into life | Choosing to step into the filth | *Future Bugs* |
| Addicted (temptation) | Post-encounter pornography temptation | *Morning Dew* |
| Addicted (body eruption) | Boar-person with barbed penis | *Breaking Out* |
| Addicted (encounter without relation) | Garden succubi demanding the seed | *Temptation In The Garden* |
| Addicted (world-structure) | Frantic depressing supermarket consumption | *Supermarket of Sin* |
| Impotent (as threat) | Depression injection offered by menacing figures | *Jesus Is Lord* |
| Impotent (as risk) | Dread and withdrawal from embodied task | *Future Bugs* |

Of all four masculine archetypes, the Lover is perhaps the most theologically fraught in this corpus — because the dreamer's spiritual tradition has historically been ambivalent about the body, sensory pleasure, and erotic experience. The diary itself does not share that ambivalence: it treats bodily aliveness as continuous with spiritual aliveness, and the Lover's full presence as something to be protected from the depression injection rather than sacrificed on the altar of a disembodied piety.
