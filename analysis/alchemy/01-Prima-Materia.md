# Prima Materia: The Raw Stuff of the Work

## Definition

The *prima materia* — "first matter" — is the chaotic, undifferentiated raw substance from which the alchemical Great Work begins. Paracelsus called it *the invisible, unformed chaos from which God created the visible world*. It is simultaneously everywhere and nowhere: *vilis et vilissima* — the cheapest and most contemptible of things, yet containing within it everything the Work requires. The adept's first task is to *recognise* the prima materia — to see the gold concealed in the dung heap.

Psychologically, the prima materia is the *massa confusa* of the unconscious: the unexamined compulsions, the unintegrated drives, the shadow projections, the instinctual energy not yet given form or direction. It is not evil — it is raw. It is not corrupt — it is unrefined. The Work does not destroy it; it transmutes it.

## The Prima Materia in This Dream Corpus

### 1. Instinctual Multiplicity: *Author, Knight, Elder*

This dream presents the prima materia in its most elemental form as a sequence of unintegrated life-identities. The *Author* (intellectual, symbolic power), the *Knight* (martial service, loss of stronghold), and the *Elder* (tribal patriarch, generative potency) do not yet constitute a unified self — they are facets of raw psychic material waiting to be integrated. The Elder's unapologetic reproductive drive — *"as a prestigious person, I was able to be gratified by any woman I liked at any time"* — is pure sulphur: unrefined libidinal energy, powerful but undirected. The invasion by centaurs, demonic boars, and demon aliens is the prima materia *attacking itself* — an image of the psyche's own contents in conflict before the Work has imposed order.

The castle — ancient seat of authority — falls. This is the necessary collapse of the existing psychic structure. Without this *nigredo*-adjacent loss, the new synthesis cannot begin.

### 2. The Body as Base Matter: *Breaking Out* (Boar Sequence)

The dreamer *becomes a boar-person*: tusks, plaited beard, horns, shaggy hair. This is one of the most explicitly alchemical images in the corpus. The boar represents raw, aggressive, earth-bound instinct — in heraldry, it is the emblem of the warrior who neither retreats nor submits. Here it is also the *anthropos in animalistic form*: the human being in its pre-differentiated, instinct-driven state, *uncertain of its own nature and origin*.

The barbed penis is a particularly striking image. It causes harm without the dreamer's knowledge or intention. This is the prima materia's danger: it wounds not out of malice but out of sheer, blunt natural force. The dreamer's immediate response — *anger, and the demand for information about his own nature* — is the alchemist's first move: the demand to *know* what one is working with.

*"What are we even called?"* — this question is the prima materia becoming self-aware.

### 3. The Counterfeit Store: *Supermarket of Sin*

The supermarket presents the prima materia in its social and spiritual dimension: *everything on the shelves is trash*. The goods of the world — pleasure, stimulation, consumption — are revealed as *counterfeit gold*: they look like nourishment but are *carcinogenic*. This is precisely the alchemical concept of the *false lapis* — the pseudo-gold that deceives the uninitiated alchemist. The prima materia is present in the desperate hunger of the customers, the toxicity of the products, and the malevolence of the hidden owner. All of this is raw psychic material needing to be replaced, shelf by shelf, with the genuine article.

### 4. The Simulation as Chaos: *EverMind*

The EverMind narrative presents perhaps the most intellectually elaborate prima materia in the corpus. The Sourceress — *an ultra-impressionable child soul completely imprinted by celebrity consumer culture* — represents the psyche in its most uncritical, appetitive, and unformed state. She builds a world of gold (*BabyLondon*) as a tribute to celebrity: a perfect image of *pseudo-rubedo*, false gold masquerading as the genuine completion of the Work.

The violence that flows from her — the torture of Calvin Trent and his sons, the repeated killing and resuscitation of the puppy Rahma — illustrates the havoc the unrefined prima materia wreaks on those within its sphere. The prima materia is not evil, but it is dangerous: it operates by appetite alone, without conscience, until the Work begins.

Crucially, the resolution comes through *CodeSong* — music and beauty rediscovered, a *coniunctio* of creativity and order. The Sourceress's self-termination, far from being tragic, is the *mortificatio* that consummates the Work: having given birth to genuine beauty, the raw, appetitive self dissolves.

### 5. Primitive Rites and Sacrifice: *Monkey Junkie*

The Norse town of monkey vikings offers another tableau of the prima materia. The hallucinogen administered to the young Viking, the ritual sacrifice, the rite of passage by wounding (*slicing his thigh*) — these are all images of instinctual, pre-rational culture attempting to manage its own raw material through ritual. The young monkey's transformation after the wounding — the speech, the grabbing of the sword, the leaping of the fence — is the prima materia *responding to the Work*: a crude initiation that, despite its brutality, produces genuine transformation.

The dreamer's role is as observer and witness — the alchemist watching the process in the vessel, not yet fully engaged but beginning to recognize what he sees.

## The Properties of the Prima Materia

Drawing together these dream-instances, the prima materia in this corpus has the following consistent properties:

- **It is bodily**: tusks, barbed flesh, hunger, lust, sensation. It is always material, always embodied.
- **It is multiple**: many identities, many voices, many factions. The *massa confusa* resists unity.
- **It is appetitive**: it wants. It devours, desires, builds, hungers.
- **It is dangerous in its ignorance**: it harms not from malice but from lack of self-knowledge.
- **It contains its own question**: *What are we even called?* The prima materia wants to know itself. This wanting is the seed of the Work.
- **It is recognisable**: the dreamer, again and again, *recognises* the prima materia — in the supermarket, in the simulation, in the boar — even when he is inside it. This recognition is the alchemist's gift.

The prima materia is not the enemy. It is the gold that has not yet found its form.
