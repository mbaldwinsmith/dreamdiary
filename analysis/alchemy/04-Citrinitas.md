# Citrinitas: The Yellowing

## Definition

*Citrinitas* — the yellowing — is the most liminal of the four alchemical stages. It was frequently omitted from later alchemical systems, absorbed into either the albedo or the rubedo; Jung gave it comparatively little attention. Yet it is a real and distinct phenomenological moment: the *dawn*. Not yet the full gold of the rubedo sun, but the first warming of the sky at the horizon — the light that says *morning is coming* without yet delivering the day.

Alchemically, the citrinitas was sometimes called *xanthosis* (Greek: yellowing) and associated with the *first emanations of the Stone* — the initial appearance of value and coherence before the full completion of the Work. In psychological terms, it corresponds to the experience of *vocation* and *prophetic clarity*: the moment when the soul, having been purified through the nigredo and albedo, begins to perceive its purpose, its role in the larger pattern, its place in the divine economy.

The citrinitas is the stage of the *herald*. Not yet the king, not yet the completed warrior — but the one who can see the king coming and begins to prepare the way.

This dream corpus has a remarkably rich and consistent citrinitas layer, unusually saturated for a personal dream diary. The dreamer is, in alchemical terms, a soul whose Work has progressed to the point where prophetic vocation has become the dominant motif.

## The Herald's Vocation: Knowing Before Arrival

### *Herald of the King*

*"Spreading the good news of the second coming. Different than expected. Children of God are literally the body of Christ and, together, form the Third Temple. The second coming will be preceded by a mass awakening of Christ's body... This means that the first stages of the Second Coming are already underway!"*

This dream is pure citrinitas: the dreamer perceives the coming Day — the rubedo of cosmic history — and his vocation is to announce it. The insight that the Second Coming is *"different than expected"* is a classic prophetic structure: the dream does not deliver the familiar religious narrative; it *reframes* it, seeing the gold where others expect a different form. The herald's task is precisely this: to see truly and say truly.

The *first stages already underway* is the citrinitas insight about itself: the dawn has already begun. The Work is not complete — the sun has not yet risen — but the light at the horizon is real.

### *Chosen Ones with the Mark*

*"I tried to say 'you have the wrong Mark' but the words caught in my throat... I pushed and bellowed, YOU HAVE THE WRONG MARK."*

The prophetic compulsion to speak truth against collective misreading is a citrinitas signature. The dreamer must correct the assembly's interpretation of the prophecy: *"there are many Chosen Ones with the mark, not just one Chosen Mark."* This is a genuine theological insight delivered under prophetic compulsion — the citrinitas speaking through the body when the intellect might remain polite. The invisible force choking the dreamer is the resistance of the collective shadow to prophetic correction.

The river with its series of gates and locks that follows is the citrinitas path: progress toward the gold is real, but there are many thresholds (*series of locks*), and the landscape shifts continuously. The dreamer navigates these gates, not by force, but by the willingness to *take a detour* when the gates are closed.

### *Divine Plans*

*"Reshaping creation according to spoken prompts... They showed me how they and the Word of God reshapes reality through the spoken and written word. Taught me how to prompt reality."*

This is the citrinitas as *prophetic apprenticeship*. The dreamer is not yet the one reshaping reality — he is being *taught the grammar of reality*. The senior angels are the citrinitas masters; the dreamer is the student approaching the threshold of the rubedo. The observation that *so little is actually prompted* — that chaos remains the default — is precisely the citrinitas insight: vast creative power exists, but it requires conscious, deliberate, prayerful *application* to be effective. The dreamer is being trained in the *Logos* — the spoken word that shapes reality — as a precondition of the rubedo's full sovereignty.

## Prophetic Vision: Seeing Through the Surface

### *Christ Cycle*

*"Types of Christ and prophets continually born, refining the type, pruning off false Messiahs, false prophets and false prophecies. Truth is distilled by the cycle."*

This is perhaps the most theologically sophisticated citrinitas insight in the corpus. The dreamer perceives history itself as an *alchemical process*: the repeated cycles of prophets and types are the successive operations — calcinatio, solutio, separatio — by which the divine truth is progressively purified from its false imitations. The image of *pruning* is explicitly alchemical: the distillation of truth requires the repeated removal of impurities (*false Messiahs, false prophecies*).

The key citrinitas insight: *"All actions on the crucifixion, even the piercing of Jesus' side, demonstrated to be unintentionally holy, prophetic acts."* The Work transforms even the instruments of darkness into instruments of purification — the very hand that wounds becomes part of the completed pattern. This is the citrinitas recognising the rubedo's retroactive transformation of the nigredo: all the blackening was, in retrospect, necessary and holy.

### *Atheist Logos*

*"Had a chat about the Logos-Sophia... I believed in the Logos while still an atheist. Trinity of Source, Logos-Sophia, and Presence/Breath. Later discovered Jesus as Logos-Sophia through the Gospel of John."*

This short but dense dream records the *intellectual* citrinitas: the gradual clarification of the divine nature before the full personal encounter. The soul, even in atheism, perceived *something* — the *Logos*, the ordering principle of reality — and named it with philosophical precision. The later identification of Jesus as the *Logos-Sophia incarnate* is the citrinitas transition to rubedo: the abstract principle takes on a face, a name, a body, a history.

## The Prophetic Burden: Citrinitas as Danger

The citrinitas is not comfortable. The dawn is cold. Prophetic clarity carries the risk of isolation, misunderstanding, and the temptation to force the Work.

### *Trumpety Trump*

*"I was awakening people, rousing their souls to sing, like a conductor. When I did too much, people started chanting 'trumpety trump' and turning on me. I had to calm them and slip away."*

This dream addresses the citrinitas danger with great precision. The dreamer has the gift of awakening — he can rouse sleeping souls to song — but there is a *threshold of tolerance*. Too much awakening at once and the collective turns hostile. The very gift that is his vocation becomes, in excess, a provocation. The *"jock types hunting for him"* to either attack or make him their leader is a double danger: the citrinitas soul risks both persecution and idolisation.

The wisdom here is in *slipping away* — not abandoning the vocation, but honouring the limits of the vessel. The citrinitas alchemist must apply heat *gradually*; too rapid a transition burns rather than refines.

### *Contingent Pain*

*"Why is there pain? Reality is contingent and cause driven, governed by laws and rules, and that pain is necessary for survival and growth. Needless pain is the price of freedom, survival and growth."*

This dream records the citrinitas at the level of *theodicy* — the justification of divine providence in a world of suffering. The dreamer gives an answer that is simultaneously philosophical and deeply personal: pain is the price of a real world, a world with genuine causation and genuine freedom. The citrinitas does not minimise darkness; it *places it within a comprehensible pattern*. The Work requires suffering not because suffering is good but because transformation requires real fire, not simulated warmth.

## The Citrinitas and the Dawning Kingdom

### *Confession of Calling to Dad*

*"Told Dad all about my calling. In return, he told me all about some artwork he'd been doing. He showed me a three-faced painting and half a bust pressed against a mirror. He mentioned Janus."*

The *Janus* image — the two-faced god of thresholds, looking simultaneously at past and future — is the quintessential symbol of the citrinitas: the *threshold state* between the old world and the new. The dreamer tells his father about his *calling* — the citrinitas vocation made explicit. The father responds with *Janus*: you are at a threshold.

The lucid period of the dream — *"I delighted in touching everything and feasting my eyes on my surroundings. I sang and moved my body"* — is the citrinitas as *embodied delight*: the senses, purified by the albedo, now receive the world with pleasure and reverence. The vast Edinburgh-like city in which this occurs is the *civitas* of the emerging rubedo — not yet arrived, but present enough to walk through in joy.

## Summary: The Shape of the Citrinitas

The citrinitas in this corpus consistently involves:

1. **Prophetic compulsion**: the soul must speak truth, correct error, announce what is coming, even against resistance
2. **Theological clarification**: abstract principles become names, names become persons, persons become encounters
3. **Perception of pattern**: history, suffering, and apparent disorder are seen as stages in a coherent divine Work
4. **The danger of excess**: too much light too quickly burns; the herald must pace the announcement
5. **Threshold consciousness**: Janus, the river locks, the series of gates — the citrinitas knows it is in between
6. **Embodied delight**: once the purification is sufficiently advanced, the senses themselves become instruments of wonder

The citrinitas is the stage of *becoming what you were always meant to be* — not yet fully arrived, but finally moving in the right direction with eyes open. It is the stage of *calling*, of *vocation*, of *prophetic service to the emerging gold*.

## The Citrinitas in the Poems

The poems are saturated with citrinitas energy — more so than any other stage. This is consistent with the dreamer's conscious life: the vocation-consciousness that characterises the citrinitas has been deliberately articulated and explored in waking composition.

*Radiant Knight* is the citrinitas vocation poem par excellence. Every element is correct for the dawning stage: the knight serves the light he has not yet fully become ("you illuminate my eyes with love"), carries the tools of the task not yet completed (the sword, the shield, the breastplate of righteousness), and is sent toward the weak and the battlefield — the herald's work, not the king's. The poem's explicit acknowledgment of vulnerability — "When under the shadow of death, / I search the darkness for Your light" — is the citrinitas's characteristic cold: it is not yet the full warmth of the rubedo, and the herald knows it. The appeal — "I long for the healing kiss of Your breath, / that renews my heart by Your might" — is the citrinitas asking for what only the rubedo can provide, while faithfully serving in the meantime.

*Pour Out My Soul*'s central declaration is the citrinitas in its most compressed form: "By holy Word I fight / so more may be reborn / I am a child of light / who sings now of the dawn." The *child of light* is not yet the *king of light* — but the dawn is real and the singing has begun. The gold bowls filling with incense at the poem's close (echoing Revelation 5:8) are the citrinitas as priestly service: the herald preparing the way through prayer, making ready the vessels for the rubedo's completion.

*Since the Beginning* presents the citrinitas from the divine side — the God who calls the soul to its vocation: "You were made with a song to sing to me. / Every one of my beloved prophets spoke / So that you would open your eyes to me / And lift up your voice in my chorus." This is the citrinitas's founding moment: the recognition that the calling is not invented by the soul but received from a source that has been preparing for this moment since before creation. "Join my dance, the song of the harvest, / And New Eden will throng with thanksgiving" — the citrinitas herald is invited to participate in the approaching rubedo, not to wait for it passively but to join its advance.

*A Name Untold*'s concluding gift — "I am given my name, the Oaken Pilgrim" — is the citrinitas's characteristic resolution: the prophetic identity clarified from outside the ego, received rather than achieved. The Oaken Pilgrim is not yet the gold of the rubedo's completion, but it is the clear sense of vocation that makes the herald's service possible. The name arrives as the culmination of the albedo's purification — the pilgrim who has been washed enough to receive the name.
