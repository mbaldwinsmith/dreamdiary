# Child Shadow Integration


## Monster Baby

* Playing with a baby
* Baby has a violent tantrum
* Questioning my Mum's lack of discipline
* Baby transforms into a monster
* Wrestle the monster
* Monster grows into a Dragon
* Dragon is too big to wrestle
* Dragon complains it has nowhere to roost


## Storming the Castle

* I am navigating a young child through a dark forest
* We approach a towering castle
* The castle is protected by a forcefield
* I know that the evil sorceress lives there
* I draw in energy and bellow at the castle
* The forcefield flickers
* The castle begins to crumble
* A knight in shining white armour jumps down
* The knight challenges me to a duel
* I fight the knight with a sword
* I best the knight
* The bested knight laughs
* Knight says while we were fighting the jester was castrating me
* Image of the jester spreading rumours about me
* Semi-lucid, I will this away but can't
* I see images confirming my lack of manhood
* I realise I've been defeated


 ## Altercation with Dad

 * I wake in a large barn, covered in cute babies
 * They're harmless and babbling
 * A pretty older woman lies next to me and looks at me fondly
 * I gentle break free of the babies and explain my nightmare
 * I explain my realisation that young children are mini-tyrants
 * Everyone is outraged
 * I stand up and my Dad storms over aggressively, fists clenched
 * He squares up to me, in my personal space, fists out
 * I put my hand on his fists and tell him I love him
 * He feels my folding comb against his leg in my pocket
 * He accuses me of carrying a knife
 * I pull it out and show him, but he snaps it in half
 * I object, but he laughs and says I'm in trouble in my school year
 * Suddenly young and panicking, I desperately try to figure out what he means


## Confronting the Twisting Shadow

* I'm in a dark corridor and walk through a door
* Shadowy bats flee the light as the door opens
* I realise I'm in a dream and suddenly fill with rage
* I storm after the bats and a shadowy figure is cornered
* The shadow writhes and convulves before me, as though my gaze hurts it
* Suddenly filled with compassion, I try to say I love you but it's muffled
* I gently approach the shadow and it recoils
* I sit down next to it and wrap my arms around myself in a hug
* I just sit, gently present with the shadow
* A voice weeps, "nobody likes me", echoing my inconsolable tears during depression as a teenager
* I cry and say "I love you", clearer this time.
* The shadow turns into a watery blob and slowly dissipates
* I wake up crying and hugging myself
