# Driving away demons
*   A Bible scholar approached me in a pub, telling me i'd got the gospel wrong
*   I invited correction
*   he started drawing on Apocrypha, possibly gnostic 'gospels' and fragments
*   I was dubious and took it with a pinch of salt
*   we were walking along a woodland path with deep, impenetrably dark woodland on either side
*   I noticed a shadowy, hulking figure following us and getting closer and turned to say a silent prayer of protection
*   my scholarly companion, still explaining away, got visibly frustrated, saying "what are you looking at, there's nothing there!" but looking as though he was worried and sweating
*   moments later, he yelped, pushed at me, and started running as the hulking figure became more solid, roared, and charged straight at us
*   the scholar broke off into the forest, leaving the path, but I stuck to the path and ran towards the light at the far end
*   looking back, more hulking figures had broken out onto the path, both huge humanoids and wolves. Occasionally, when they were gaining on me, i'd turn and call out to Jesus to protect me and they'd be flung back and stunned
*   eventually I reached the light, which turned out to be a peaceful, candlelit graveyard
*   I had no clear path left and a huge wolf was still charging at me. I fell to my knees, facing back towards the path and just prayed
*   through my eyelids, I saw a burst of light and a beautiful chorus of song broke out from all around me, when I opened my eyes, the wolf was fleeing into the distance and I was surrounded by indistinct figures of singing light

# The city
*   After the confrontation, I was in a partially ruined city with riots and fortified checkpoints
*   I saw Rupert Charkham on the other side of a security fence and called out to him, hoping to talk to him about the demonic attack, but he either didn't hear me or ignored me
*   as I walked the ruins, cut off from getting home by apologetic guards, I reflected that Rupert and co were going back to the comfort of their homes when they should be out here
*   I wished peace upon a man that was shouting aggressively at the guards
*   at one point I found myself at a meeting for those affected by the disaster headed by Molly Simms
*   she asked which family I was with. I said that I was on my own and that I climbed through the hole in the wall
*   I asked the refugees whether they'd seen anything strange, to nods and murmurs of assent. I told them about the huge men and the big blue wolf.
*   as I left, I told them to remember who is really in charge. God!
*   I regretted not telling them to explicitly call on the name of Jesus

# The bird
*   walking through a supermarket, wading through waist-high plastic beads, trying to follow ones of particular colours, like on my spirit walks
*   as the colours increased, it became overwhelming and impossible to know which way to go
*   unsure what to do, knowing it was a dream, I started eating the beads
*   a redheaded woman stormed or glided at me, shouting at me to have some respect
*   the dream and all of the people in it then started to turn on me
*   I turned and faced them, ready to be torn apart
*   instead, the woman stopped, looked at me and told me to be more respectful
*   she introduced herself as Ginger
*   I apologised, said that she was right, that I'd been disrespectful and that I was grateful for correction
*   she turned into a little bird and a stiff hard leather flask, completely empty, fell beside her
*   I told her to feed on me and held out my hand to her
*   red, strawberry-like growths grew from the flesh of my fingers and she picked them off with her beak
*   it didn't hurt but I could feel her beak scratching at my finger
*   after a while, it grew more uncomfortable and I shook her off, saying that she'd had enough. She made no attempt to get more
*   I didn't feel at all weakened or wounded by the experience
