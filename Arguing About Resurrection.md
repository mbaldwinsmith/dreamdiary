* Statue of Jesus with a dark mantle of stars and space. His human body, where exposed, gleamed with soft ethereal light
* overheard a learned Englishman, probably CofE, decrying the lack of theological diversity in the church today
* I laughed that, in some ways, it's probably a blessing, that at least we don't have to endure as many 'Christians' who don't believe in the resurrection
* Most people assented, but a couple snorted, with my locutor saying that wasn't a very tolerant outlook and that it was all very complicated
* I said that such beliefs explicitly contradict our founding credal statements and that it's really very simple: either Jesus is who He says He is or He isn't
* another man, to my left, also very learned and sharply dressed, remarked that I was getting awfully upset by this
* I replied, of course I am, we're talking about the salvation of living souls. I would prefer that everyone is saved?
* My original conversation partner said, acusationally: "So, do you think I'm saved?"
* "I don't know, it isn't my decision, but I do hope so!"
* "Can I just say one more thing, then I promise I'll shut up." I said, to a murmur of assent. "In all of this tip toeing around, trying to avoid causing offense, we must watch out that we're not offending the only One Whose opinion actually matters."
